## 2. Weekly Content Production Workflow

### 2.1 Monday: Planning & Research

**9:00 AM - Content Planning Meeting (30 min)**
- Review previous week's performance
- Plan current week's topics
- Assign writing/research tasks
- Check resource availability

**Topics to Cover:**
- Blog post topic for week
- Social media themes
- Video tutorial focus
- Newsletter content

**Output:**
- Weekly content plan document
- Assigned tasks with deadlines
- Keyword research completed

### 2.2 Tuesday-Thursday: Creation & Development

**Daily Standup (9:15 AM, 15 min)**
- Progress updates
- Blockers identification
- Resource needs

**Content Creation Schedule:**

**Tuesday:**
- Blog post draft (800-1200 words)
- Social media thread outline (Twitter/LinkedIn)
- Video script draft

**Wednesday:**
- Blog post technical review
- Social media content creation
- Video recording/editing
- Newsletter content collection

**Thursday:**
- Final blog post edits
- SEO optimization
- Social media scheduling
- Video final edits
- Newsletter assembly

### 2.3 Friday: Review & Publishing

**Content Review Meeting (10:00 AM, 60 min)**
- Final content review
- Quality assurance check
- SEO validation
- Accessibility compliance

**Publishing Schedule:**
- **11:00 AM:** Blog post published
- **12:00 PM:** Social media posts scheduled
- **1:00 PM:** Video published (YouTube, website)
- **2:00 PM:** Newsletter sent
- **3:00 PM:** Documentation updates deployed

**Analytics Review (4:00 PM, 30 min)**
- Review day's performance
- Note engagement patterns
- Plan weekend social engagement

### 2.4 Weekend: Engagement & Monitoring

**Social Media Engagement:**
- Respond to comments/questions
- Share user-generated content
- Monitor discussions
- Engage with community

**Performance Monitoring:**
- Track initial engagement metrics
- Note any issues or feedback
- Prepare Monday review data

## 4. Content Types & Specifications

### 4.1 Blog Posts

**Frequency:** 1 per week (minimum)
**Length:** 800-1200 words
**Structure:**
- Engaging title (includes primary keyword)
- 1-2 sentence hook
- 3-5 sections with H2 headings
- Call-to-action at end
- Related posts links

**SEO Requirements:**
- Meta title: 50-60 characters
- Meta description: 150-160 characters
- Primary keyword in first 100 words
- 2-3 internal links
- Image alt text with keywords

### 4.2 Social Media Content

**Platforms:** Twitter, LinkedIn, Reddit (r/spreadsheets)
**Frequency:**
- Twitter: 3-5 tweets daily
- LinkedIn: 1-2 posts daily
- Reddit: 2-3 posts weekly

**Content Mix:**
- 40% Educational (tips, tutorials)
- 30% Promotional (features, updates)
- 20% Engaging (questions, polls)
- 10% Community (user content, events)

**Format Guidelines:**
- Include relevant hashtags
- Use emojis sparingly
- Add images/videos when possible
- Tag relevant accounts/people

### 4.3 Video Content

**Frequency:** 1 per week
**Length:** 5-10 minutes
**Formats:**
- Tutorials (step-by-step)
- Demos (feature showcases)
- Interviews (user/team)
- Explainer animations

**Platforms:**
- YouTube (primary)
- Website embed
- Social media clips (1-2 min)

**Requirements:**
- HD quality (1080p minimum)
- Clear audio
- Subtitles/closed captions
- Transcript provided
- Thumbnail with branding

### 4.4 Email Newsletter

**Frequency:** Weekly (Friday afternoon)
**Audience:** Subscribers, users, prospects
**Structure:**
- Subject: [Week of Date] + Benefit
- Opening: Personal greeting + week highlight
- Content: 3-5 items (blog, video, update, tip)
- CTA: Primary action (read, watch, try)
- Footer: Social links, unsubscribe

**Metrics to Track:**
- Open rate (target: >25%)
- Click-through rate (target: >3%)
- Unsubscribe rate (target: <0.5%)
- Conversion rate (target: >1%)

### 4.5 Documentation Updates

**Frequency:** As needed, minimum monthly
**Types:**
- New feature documentation
- Tutorial updates
- API reference additions
- Troubleshooting guides

**Process:**
1. Technical writer creates draft
2. Engineering review for accuracy
3. UX review for clarity
4. Publish with version tracking
5. Announce in changelog

## 5. Content Repurposing Workflow

### 5.1 Source → Multiple Formats

**White Paper Example:**
```
Original: "Rate-Based Change Mechanics" (20 pages)
→ Blog Post: "How POLLN Learns from Your Changes" (1000 words)
→ Social Thread: 5-tweet thread explaining key concepts
→ Video: 8-minute animated explanation
→ Newsletter: Summary with key takeaways
→ Documentation: Technical implementation guide
```

**Blog Post Example:**
```
Original: "Universal Cell Architecture Explained" (1200 words)
→ Social Media: 3 infographics with key points
→ Video: 5-minute screen recording demo
→ Newsletter: Excerpt with link to full post
→ Documentation: Architecture overview section
→ Slide Deck: For sales/partner presentations
```

## 7. Performance Tracking & Optimization

### 7.1 Key Metrics Dashboard

- Website traffic (sessions, users)
- Blog post views (top 5 posts)
- Social media engagement (likes, shares, comments)
- Video views and completion rates
- Newsletter performance (opens, clicks)
- Documentation usage (page views, search terms)

**Monthly Metrics (Review first week of month):**
- Conversion rates (visitor → signup)
- SEO rankings (primary keywords)
- Audience growth (email list, social followers)
- Content production volume
- Team velocity (content completed vs planned)

**Quarterly Metrics (Review quarter end):**
- Business impact (signups attributed to content)
- Cost per acquisition (content marketing)
- Return on investment
- Competitive analysis
- Strategy adjustment decisions

### 7.2 A/B Testing Schedule

**Monthly Tests:**
- **Week 1:** Headline variations (blog posts)
- **Week 2:** CTA button text/color (website)
- **Week 3:** Email subject lines (newsletter)
- **Week 4:** Social media post timing

**Testing Methodology:**
1. Hypothesis formation
2. Test design (control vs variation)
3. Implementation (split traffic)
4. Data collection (minimum 100 conversions)
5. Analysis and decision
6. Implementation of winner

### 7.3 Quarterly Strategy Review

**Review Process:**
1. **Data Analysis:** All quarterly metrics
2. **Competitive Analysis:** Competitor content strategies
3. **Team Retrospective:** What worked, what didn't
4. **Stakeholder Feedback:** Sales, support, engineering input
5. **Strategy Adjustment:** Plan for next quarter

**Output:**
- Quarterly performance report
- Updated content strategy
- Revised editorial calendar
- Resource allocation adjustments

## 9. Risk Management & Contingency

### 9.1 Common Risks & Mitigations

**Risk: Content Production Delays**
- **Mitigation:** Buffer in schedule, backup content
- **Contingency:** Repurpose existing content, guest posts

**Risk: Technical Inaccuracies**
- **Mitigation:** Multiple review stages, subject matter expert involvement
- **Contingency:** Correction process, version updates

**Risk: Low Engagement**
- **Mitigation:** A/B testing, audience research, quality focus
- **Contingency:** Content refresh, promotion adjustment

**Risk: Resource Constraints**
- **Mitigation:** Prioritization, automation, outsourcing
- **Contingency:** Focus on high-impact content, reduce frequency

### 9.2 Crisis Communication Plan

**Issues Requiring Immediate Response:**
- Technical errors in documentation
- Security/privacy concerns
- Negative viral content
- Platform outages

**Response Protocol:**
1. **Assessment:** Impact level determination
2. **Communication:** Internal team notification
3. **Action:** Content correction/removal
4. **Public Response:** Transparent communication
5. **Follow-up:** Prevention measures

### 9.3 Backup Content Library

**Maintain 2-4 weeks of backup content:**
- Evergreen blog posts (ready to publish)
- Social media content calendar
- Newsletter templates
- Video ideas list

**Update quarterly:**
- Review and refresh backup content
- Ensure relevance and accuracy
- Update seasonal references
