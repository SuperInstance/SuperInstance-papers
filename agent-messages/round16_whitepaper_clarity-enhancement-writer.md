### 2. Clarity Issues Taxonomy
**Conceptual Confusion:**
- Abstract theories without grounding
- Mathematical notation without meaning
- Technical terms without definition
- Complex relationships unexplained

**Structural Confusion:**
- Logical jumps without bridges
- Missing context for conclusions
- Unclear section purposes
- Poor example integration

**Presentation Confusion:**
- Dense paragraph formatting
- Overwhelming equation blocks
- Unclear diagram references
- Minimal visual hierarchy

### 3. Rewriting Strategy

**Before → After Transformation Process:**

**Abstract → Concrete:**
*Before:* "The tensor field exhibits non-trivial topological properties"
*After:* "Imagine stretching a rubber sheet - the way it bends and curves shows its shape properties. Our data acts like this sheet, with specific bending patterns we can measure."

**Jargon → Plain Language:**
*Before:* "Utilizing higher-order combinatory logic"
*After:* "Using a system that combines simple building blocks to create complex operations"

**Sudden Leap → Gradual Bridge:**
*Before:* "Therefore x=5. QED"
*After:* "Let me show you how we get there: first we see that combining these relationships gives us a simpler equation. From there, solving step by step leads us to the answer: x=5."

**Equation-Heavy → Concept-First:**
*Before:* Presenting full derivation
*After:* "Here's what's happening in plain terms: we're combining like quantities. The mathematical proof below confirms what this reasoning suggests."

### 4. Clarity Enhancement Techniques

**The Trailer Method:**
"What we're about to see is like a recipe with three ingredients..."

**The Map Method:**
"Let's preview our journey: we start with the basics, add complexity one layer at a time, and arrive at our final insight."

**The Spotlight Method:**
"Here's the key insight (pause to let it sink in): ..."

**The Compare Method:**
"Think of this like [familiar concept], except for one crucial difference: ..."
