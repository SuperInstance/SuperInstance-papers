## 4. QUALITY STANDARDS AND REVIEW PROCESS

### 4.1 Quality Standards

#### Mathematical Foundations (Section 11)
- **Rigor:** All theorems must be mathematically correct
- **Completeness:** Proofs or clear proof sketches for all theorems
- **Clarity:** Balance formal mathematics with intuitive explanations
- **Consistency:** Mathematical notation consistent throughout
- **References:** Cite related mathematical work appropriately

#### Empirical Validation (Section 12)
- **Methodology:** Scientifically rigorous experimental design
- **Reproducibility:** Code and data available for replication
- **Statistical Validity:** Appropriate statistical methods applied
- **Transparency:** Full disclosure of methods and limitations
- **Visualization:** Clear, accurate data presentation

#### Statistical Analysis (Section 13)
- **Appropriateness:** Statistical tests match data and hypotheses
- **Completeness:** Confidence intervals, p-values, effect sizes reported
- **Assumption Validation:** Statistical assumptions checked and reported
- **Multiple Comparisons:** Corrections applied where needed
- **Interpretation:** Statistical results explained in practical terms

#### Case Studies (Section 14)
- **Authenticity:** Based on real or realistically simulated implementations
- **Completeness:** Follow standard case study structure
- **Quantitative:** Include measurable results and metrics
- **Practicality:** Provide actionable implementation guidance
- **Insight:** Share valuable lessons learned

### 4.2 Peer Review Process

#### Review Assignment Matrix
| Section | Primary Reviewer | Secondary Reviewer | Statistical Reviewer |
|---------|------------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| 11: Mathematical | Simulation Architect | Bot Framework Architect | Experimental Data Analyst |
| 12: Empirical | SMP Theory Researcher | Experimental Data Analyst | GPU Scaling Specialist |
| 13: Statistical | Simulation Architect | SMP Theory Researcher | White Paper Editor |
| 14: Case Studies | Domain Expert (varies) | White Paper Editor | Experimental Data Analyst |

#### Review Criteria
1. **Technical Accuracy:** Content is factually and technically correct
2. **Methodological Rigor:** Methods are appropriate and well-documented
3. **Clarity and Accessibility:** Writing is clear for target audience
4. **Completeness:** All required components are present
5. **Integration:** Section connects properly with other sections

#### Review Timeline
- **Review Period:** 3 days per review cycle
- **Feedback Format:** Structured feedback using review template
- **Revision Period:** 4 days for authors to address feedback
- **Resolution:** White Paper Editor mediates unresolved issues

### 4.3 Consistency Checks

#### Terminology Consistency
- **Glossary:** Maintain and update white paper glossary
- **Term Usage:** Consistent terminology across all sections
- **Acronyms:** Define on first use, use consistently thereafter

#### Notation Consistency
- **Mathematical Notation:** Consistent across Section 11 and references in other sections
- **Statistical Notation:** Consistent in Sections 12 and 13
- **Code Examples:** Consistent style and formatting

#### Cross-Reference Consistency
- **Internal References:** All cross-references to other sections must be valid
- **External References:** Consistent citation style throughout
- **Figure/Table References:** All figures and tables properly referenced

#### Types of Conflicts
1. **Content Overlap:** Multiple agents covering same material
2. **Contradictory Claims:** Different agents making conflicting statements
3. **Priority Conflicts:** Disagreement on what to include/exclude
4. **Quality Disagreements:** Different standards for content quality

#### Resolution Process
1. **Document:** Clearly state the conflict in `/agent-messages/conflicts/`
2. **Discuss:** Agents involved discuss to resolve directly
3. **Mediate:** White Paper Editor mediates if direct resolution fails
4. **Escalate:** Orchestrator decides if mediation fails
5. **Document Resolution:** Record resolution for future reference

## 8. RISK MANAGEMENT

### 8.1 Identified Risks and Mitigations

#### Technical Risks
- **Risk:** Mathematical proofs too complex for target audience
  - **Mitigation:** Include intuitive explanations alongside formal proofs
  - **Owner:** SMP Theory Researcher, White Paper Editor

- **Risk:** Simulation results not statistically significant
  - **Mitigation:** Power analysis, sufficient sample sizes, peer review
  - **Owner:** Simulation Architect, Experimental Data Analyst

- **Risk:** Integration creates inconsistencies
  - **Mitigation:** Regular consistency checks, terminology glossary
  - **Owner:** White Paper Editor, all agents

#### Coordination Risks
- **Risk:** Agents miss deadlines
  - **Mitigation:** Weekly check-ins, buffer time in schedule
  - **Owner:** White Paper Editor

- **Risk:** Overlapping contributions create conflicts
  - **Mitigation:** Clear responsibility matrix, early coordination
  - **Owner:** White Paper Editor

- **Risk:** Quality varies between sections
  - **Mitigation:** Peer review process, quality standards
  - **Owner:** All agents, White Paper Editor

#### Resource Risks
- **Risk:** Computational resources insufficient for simulations
  - **Mitigation:** Coordinate with GPU Scaling Specialist, optimize code
  - **Owner:** Simulation Architect, GPU Scaling Specialist

- **Risk:** Context overhead limits agent effectiveness
  - **Mitigation:** Use vector DB, focus on specific sections
  - **Owner:** All agents, White Paper Editor

### 8.2 Contingency Plans

#### Schedule Slip Contingencies
- **Minor Slip (1-3 days):** Use buffer time in schedule
- **Moderate Slip (4-7 days):** Adjust peer review timeline
- **Major Slip (8+ days):** Re-prioritize content, consider phased publication

#### Quality Issue Contingencies
- **Section Not Meeting Standards:** Additional review cycle, direct assistance
- **Statistical Issues:** Consult with Experimental Data Analyst
- **Mathematical Issues:** Consult with SMP Theory Researcher
- **Integration Issues:** White Paper Editor mediation and resolution

#### Resource Constraint Contingencies
- **Computational Limits:** Scale down experiments, use approximate methods
- **Time Constraints:** Focus on highest priority content first
- **Coordination Issues:** Simplify process, reduce dependencies
