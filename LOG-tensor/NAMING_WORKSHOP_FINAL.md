## Executive Summary

This document presents a comprehensive naming analysis comparing two primary candidates for our tensor architecture:

1. **LOG-Tensor** (Ledger-Origin-Geometry)
2. **Rubiks-Tensor** (Permutation-Constraint Tensor)

After rigorous analysis across five dimensions—first impressions, semantics, mathematical resonance, brand strength, and scoring framework—this workshop recommends **LOG-Tensor** as the primary naming candidate with a secondary "Rubiks-style" descriptor for the permutation constraint mechanism.

**Key Finding:** While "Rubiks-Tensor" offers visual memorability, "LOG-Tensor" better captures the system's core innovation: the ledger-based origin-relative coordinate system with geometric operations. The "Rubiks" paradigm is better positioned as a *property* of LOG-Tensors rather than their identity.

## Part 3: Mathematical Resonance Analysis

### 3.1 Does "Rubiks" Accurately Capture Our Permutation Math?

**The Rubik's Cube Group:**

The mathematical structure of a Rubik's cube is:
$$G = (C_3^7 \rtimes S_8) \times (C_2^{11} \rtimes S_{12}) / K$$

Where:
- $C_3^7$: Corner orientations (7 corners, 3 orientations each)
- $S_8$: Corner permutations (8 corners)
- $C_2^{11}$: Edge orientations (11 edges, 2 orientations each)
- $S_{12}$: Edge permutations (12 edges)
- $K$: Kernel from constraints

**Our System's Permutation Structure:**

Our tensor system has:
- **Dependency graphs:** Modifications propagate through dependency networks
- **Parity constraints:** Total parity must be preserved
- **Sector permutations:** Base-12 cyclic group $C_{12}$ for sector rotations
- **Orientation encoding:** Baked into tensor placement

**Alignment Score: 7/10**

**What Aligns:**
- Permutation dependencies ✓
- Constraint satisfaction ✓
- Named encodings (algorithms) ✓
- Transformation groups ✓

**What Doesn't Align:**
- Rubik's cube is a specific permutation group; our system is more general
- Our system has continuous parameters; Rubik's cube is discrete
- Our system has origin-relative coordinates; Rubik's cube has fixed center

**Conclusion:** "Rubiks" captures the *style* of permutation dependencies but not the specific mathematics. It's an analogy, not a formal connection.

**Ledger Concept:**

Our system maintains a "ledger" of operations:

**Mathematical Formulation:**

Our coordinate system is origin-relative:

All positions measured relative to a reference point (the "self").

**Geometry Concept:**

Our operations are geometric:

**Alignment Score: 9/10**

**What Aligns:**
